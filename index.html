About Paris ACTS

Paris ACTS has been created to reinforce the links between different research groups in the Paris area which are working on the connections between automata theory and concurrency theory, and in order to advance this research on a national and international level,

The seminar takes place every six to eight weeks, moving between LIX (École Polytechnique), LRE (EPITA Paris), IRIF (Université Paris Cité), LMF (Université Paris-Saclay), SAMOVAR (Télécom SudParis), and LACL (Université Paris Est Créteil). It is held in hybrid mode and has a slight thematic overlap with the MeFoSyLoMa seminar.

The seminar has a low-traffic mailing list for announcements etc. If you want to join, send an email to p-acts-owner@ml.lre.epita.fr.

The general program for each seminar is as follows:

14:00--14:45 In-person talk
14:45--15:30 Virtual or in-person talk
15:30--16:00 Break
16:00--17:00 Discussion

Next Seminar

8 January 2026

Venue: LRE, EPITA Paris

Speaker 1: (present) Samy Abbes, IRIF

Title: Probabilistic Concurrent Systems

► Abstract:

We investigate the construction of memoryless probability measures for trace monoids, a simple probabilistic and stateless model of concurrency. Although being stateless makes obviously trace monoids poor candidates for real-life models of concurrency, it turns out that a fair amount of the specificity of mixing probability and concurrency already appears in these models. It is henceforth a valuable step toward the study of more realistic probabilistic models of concurrency to have a good understanding of memoryless probability measures for trace monoids. The general idea is to transpose to trace monoids the standard model of coin tossing for words. As the combinatorics of trace monoids is strongly involved in this study, the Möbius polynomials of trace monoids and other related objects of linear algebra become our central objects of investigation.

Speaker 2: (present) Yorgo Chamoun, École polytechnique

Title: Realizations of Relational Presheaves

► Abstract:

Relational presheaves generalize traditional presheaves by going to the category of sets and relations (as opposed to sets and functions) and by allowing functors which are lax. This added generality is useful because it intuitively allows one to encode situations where we have representables without boundaries or with multiple boundaries at once. In particular, the relational generalization of precubical sets has natural application to modeling concurrency. We study categories of relational presheaves, and construct realization functors for those. We begin by observing that they form the category of set-based models of a cartesian theory, which implies in particular that they are locally finitely presentable categories. By using general results from categorical logic, we then show that the realization of such presheaves in a cocomplete category is a model of the theory in the opposite category, which allows characterizing situations in which we have a realization functor. Finally, we explain that our work has applications in the semantics of concurrency theory.
